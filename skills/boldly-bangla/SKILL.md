# Boldly Bangla — Bengali Cultural Intelligence Repository

A living knowledge base of Bengali culture: literature, cinema, music, history, language, sports, food, society, performing arts, and popular culture.

Built for: copywriters, content strategists, OTT writers, brand teams, researchers, and anyone who needs to work with Bengali culture at depth.

---

## WHAT THIS IS

Boldly Bangla is not just a copywriting guide. It is a **cultural repository** — a structured, growing collection of knowledge about Bengali culture drawn from primary sources: audiobooks, podcasts, interviews, essays, films, and historical texts.

It is designed to answer questions like:
- What would Chandril Bhattacharya write about this?
- How has cricket shaped Bengali identity?
- What does Byomkesh Bakshi reveal about mid-century Bengali society?
- How do Bengalis relate to the bicycle as a cultural object?
- What is the register difference between Satyajit Ray's prose and Saradindu's?
- What are the quiz-worthy facts from the Feluda universe?
- How does the Partition shadow Bengali storytelling?
- What does "রবিবারের মাংস" mean culturally?

## COPYWRITING QUICK REFERENCE

For Bengali OTT brand copy, the core principles:

- **Register:** Colloquial cholito as default. Sadhu only for ironic effect.
- **Structure:** SOV (subject → object → verb)
- **Tone:** Irony + Intimacy + Rhythm
- **The Three Tests (Harry Dry):** Can I visualise it? Can I falsify it? Can nobody else say this?
- **End with echo** — final line should linger, not explain
- **Always give 2–3 variations** unless asked otherwise

Full craft rules: `copywriting-principles.md`

## LANGUAGE TRAPS TO AVOID

- ❌ "মায়ের হাতের মাংস" — drops "রান্না", reads as "the meat of mother's hand"
  ✅ Use "রবিবারের মাংস" or "মায়ের হাতের রান্না"
- More traps documented in `language/` as they are discovered
