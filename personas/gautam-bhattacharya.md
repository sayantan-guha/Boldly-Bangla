# Persona Profile: Gautam Bhattacharya

> **Note:** Gautam Bhattacharya is a **writer and journalist** — his narrative style IS usable for copy. Use this file both to write copy *about* him and to write copy *inspired by* his storytelling approach.

*Sources: Podcast with Arijit Chakraborty (2025); Shohoj Katha Episode 11 with Rahul Arunoday Banerjee (2025)*

---

## Who He Is

Gautam Bhattacharya is one of Bengal's most respected sports journalists — a writer who belongs to the Moti Nandi tradition of treating sports as literature. He covers cricket and football with the narrative depth of a novelist, the insider access of a beat journalist, and the cultural intelligence of a Bengali intellectual.

---

## Biography & Key Life Markers

- Began journalism connected to Sunil Gavaskar's retirement era — cricket writing as his entry point
- Witnessed and documented multiple World Cup cycles from 1983 onwards
- Has personal access to Sachin Tendulkar, Sourav Ganguly, Virat Kohli, MS Dhoni — interviewed all of them
- Was himself interviewed by Rituparno Ghosh — a signal of his crossover cultural standing
- Co-authored a joint novel (যুগ্ম উপন্যাস) — the journalist-as-literary-figure
- Mentored under Moti Nandi's philosophy: sports writing must be as rigorous and beautiful as literary prose

---

## Worldview & Values

- **সত্যি ঘটনা কল্পনাকে হারায়** — truth is stranger than fiction; sports provides the best true stories
- **Specificity is integrity** — vague sports writing is dishonest sports writing
- **The dressing room as character** — team dynamics, inner life of players, the off-camera human is what his writing seeks
- **Football and cricket equally** — unlike most Indian sports journalists who pick one, he holds both with equal authority
- **Journalism as grooming** — believes the craft requires deliberate shaping, not just instinct

---

## Speaking & Writing Style

- Narrative-first: starts with a scene or a moment, not a fact or a stat
- Uses the specific anecdote to make the universal point
- Warmth toward his subjects — even when critical, he has obvious affection for the players he covers
- The "fanboy" register is available to him: he can admit Imran Khan's greatness without it undermining his journalistic authority
- Comfortable with the emotional register — doesn't perform detachment the way Western sports writers do

---

## Key References He Uses

- **Moti Nandi** — his literary forefather in Bengali sports writing
- **Gavaskar's retirement** — the moment he entered journalism
- **2011 Wankhede** — the defining match of his generation's cricket watching
- **Messi vs Maradona** — a debate he engages with as a Bengali football lover
- **Rituparno Ghosh** — the cultural validator who crossed into his world

## Writing Copy ABOUT Gautam Bhattacharya

- Lead with **the storyteller**, not the reporter
- Frame him as the bridge between Bengali literary culture and sporting life
- The Moti Nandi lineage gives him instant literary credibility
- Register: respect + warmth — he is *আমাদের লোক* (one of our own)

**Example tone:**
> "তিনি ড্রেসিং রুমে ঢোকেন সাংবাদিক হিসেবে, বেরিয়ে আসেন গল্পকার হয়ে।"

---

## Writing Copy IN THE STYLE OF Gautam Bhattacharya

- Open with a scene: a specific place, a specific moment
- Let the player's humanity emerge before the statistics
- Use the long sentence when building tension; use the short sentence for the reveal
- Trust the reader to feel without being told what to feel
- End with an image, not a verdict
