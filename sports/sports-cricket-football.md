## CRICKET — The Big Moments Bengalis Carry

### 1983 World Cup — Kapil Dev's Catch
The first World Cup win. Kapil Dev's over-the-shoulder catch to dismiss Viv Richards at Lord's is one of Indian cricket's founding myths. For Bengalis of a certain age, this is where cricket became *ours*.
- **Trigger for:** Heritage, against-the-odds triumph, the underdog winning

### Sachin Tendulkar — The God
Sachin is not fandom — he is faith. Bengali reverence for Sachin transcends region or rivalry. His retirement in 2013 (at Wankhede) was a national grief event.
- Key copy hooks: "চোখ বুঁজে বলা যায়" (can be said with eyes closed), "সচিন মানে ক্রিকেট, ক্রিকেট মানে সচিন"
- **Trigger for:** Timelessness, perfection, something that belongs to everyone

### Sourav Ganguly — The Lord's Shirt
July 2002, NatWest Trophy final win. Sourav removes his jersey at Lord's balcony. The most potent single image of Bengali sporting pride.
- Key copy hooks: "Lord's-এ জার্সি খুলে দেখিয়ে দিয়েছিলেন", "দাদা"
- **Trigger for:** Unapologetic pride, Bengali defiance, doing it on the world stage

### The Chappell Episode — Comeback Narrative
Greg Chappell dropped Sourav. Sourav returned, scored runs, outlasted Chappell. The Bengali comeback myth.
- **Trigger for:** Resilience, শেষ হাসি (last laugh), প্রত্যাবর্তন (return)

### MS Dhoni — The 2011 Six
April 2, 2011. Dhoni hits the winning six at Wankhede. India wins the World Cup. "Where were you?" is a live question for every Bengali who watched it.
- Dhoni's retirement announcement (August 15, 2020 — Independence Day) was treated as a national moment of mourning + celebration
- Key copy hooks: "সেই ছক্কা", "ধোনি অবসর নিলেন যেদিন"
- **Trigger for:** Culmination, perfect ending, earned satisfaction

### Virat Kohli — The Intensity Generation
Kohli represents the post-Sachin/Sourav era — raw aggression, fitness obsession, public emotion. Bengalis respect him but don't feel the same tribal ownership.
- Key contrast: Sachin = divine calm, Sourav = Bengali pride, Kohli = millennial intensity
- **Trigger for:** Drive, relentlessness, modern ambition

### IPL — The Split
IPL (2008 onwards) created a cultural fracture: cricket as spectacle vs cricket as craft. Kolkata Knight Riders gives Bengalis a tribal IPL identity.
- Eden Gardens as the loudest stadium — a point of pride
- **Trigger for:** Energy, scale, new-India ambition; OR (ironically) what cricket lost

## FOOTBALL — Bengal's First Love

### Mohun Bagan 1911 — The Origin Myth
Mohun Bagan beat a British team to win the IFA Shield — barefoot Bengali players defeating the colonial rulers. This is the original Bengali sporting pride story, predating cricket's dominance.
- **Trigger for:** Indigenous pride, David vs Goliath, করে দেখানো (proving by doing)

### Mohun Bagan vs East Bengal Derby
The মোহনবাগান-ইস্টবেঙ্গল derby is not sport — it is identity war. It divides families, neighborhoods, generations. No neutral ground.
- Mohun Bagan = traditional, old Kolkata, establishment
- East Bengal = refugee identity, newcomers, বাস্তুহারা legacy
- **Trigger for:** Maximum tribal emotion — use only with deep cultural knowledge

### Indian Football's Decline — The Mourning
Bengali football culture carries a permanent undertone of mourning — what could have been, what was lost. P.K. Banerjee, Chuni Goswami — names invoked with reverence.
- **Trigger for:** Nostalgia, lost greatness, bittersweet memory

### Messi vs Maradona — The Bengali Debate
Bengalis are football obsessed and have been arguing Messi vs Maradona for decades. This is a living debate, not a settled question.
- Maradona's death (2020) hit Bengali football lovers hard
- **Trigger for:** The eternal debate, পক্ষ নেওয়া (taking sides), passion over reason

## SPORTS JOURNALISM AS CULTURE

### Moti Nandi — The Literary Foundation
*Striker*, *Stopper* — Moti Nandi wrote football novels. Bengali sports writing has a literary tradition most other cultures don't. Sports journalism here = narrative craft, not just reporting.
- **Trigger for:** Cultural depth, ক্লাস, treating the everyday as worthy of literature

### Eden Gardens — The Temple
Eden Gardens is not a stadium — it is a pilgrimage site. 100,000 people, the noise, the চোলাই (atmosphere) — it has its own mythology.
- **Trigger for:** Scale, collective experience, something larger than yourself
